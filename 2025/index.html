Natural Language Processing & AI for Science

We study how large language models understand, generate, and evaluate scientific text, with a focus on multilinguality, reliability, and human-centered evaluation.

About the Lab

The Zhao NLP Lab explores natural language processing and large language models with an emphasis on how AI can support and transform the scientific process. Our research spans figure-to-text generation, scientific summarization, multilingual evaluation, and methods for making model behavior more transparent and controllable.

We work at the intersection of language technologies, human-centered evaluation, and scientific communication, building datasets, tools, and benchmarks that enable more robust and accessible AI systems for researchers and practitioners across domains.

Research Themes

LLMs & the Scientific Process

We investigate how large language models can support scientific workflows, including figure-to-text generation, error detection, summarization of scientific findings, and tools for enhancing the reproducibility of research.

Cross-Temporality of LLMs

Temporality is a foundation for dependable reasoning. This, however, is a property of AI models that has been understudied. We focus on research at the frontier of the current understanding and evaluation of the temporal reasoning ability of Large Language Models.

Evaluation, Benchmarks & Reliability

We develop structured benchmarks and human-in-the-loop evaluation workflows to study model errors, interpretability, and robustness. This includes datasets for scientific figures, numerical reasoning, and multi-step inference.
